THOUGHTS ON GOALS, GROWTH & THE GAME

Finisher's Mind

What I Learn From Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi is more than a football player to me. He is a lesson in how to think on the pitch.

Watching him play, you see how calm he is. He reads the game before anyone else moves. He positions himself well and already knows where the ball is going. What I learn most is composure. When Messi has the ball, he never panics. He takes his time, finds the space, and finishes.

That is what I try to do every game. When I get a chance, I remind myself: stay calm, trust your technique, and do the job. Composure gives you everything. Without it, speed and skill don’t matter.

How I Improve My Decision-Making and Dribbling

As a striker, your decision has to be fast and right. Do I shoot? Do I pass? Do I hold the ball and wait?

To get better, I watch match footage and ask myself what I would do in that situation. It trains my brain to see options quicker. For dribbling, it is all about control and timing. I practice drills every day to sharpen my first touch and my ability to beat defenders with pace.

But I have learned one rule: never dribble without a plan. If you just run at a defender with no purpose you will lose the ball. I dribble to create space, to beat my man, and to get a shot off. Every move has to have a reason.

My Life As A Striker

Being a striker comes with pressure. Everyone expects you to score.

Playing for Mexican Girls has taught me patience, focus, and mental strength. There are games where I miss chances and nothing seems to go right. I used to let that get in my head. Now I treat it as a test of character.

If I miss, I do not dwell on it. I shake it off and wait for the next opportunity. A striker with a short memory for mistakes and a long memory for goals is dangerous. That is the mindset I am building every game.

Finisher's Mind

My Journey As A Striker

My journey has not been easy. I had to work hard to earn my position and prove I belonged.

At first, I focused only on finishing. But as I kept training, I started to understand the game better. Positioning, movement off the ball, timing my runs, it all matters as much as the shot itself.

Lionel Messi is my role model because he showed me that size does not matter if your mind is strong. My goal now is simple: score as many goals as I can, keep my composure in front of goal, and make smarter decisions every game.
